# NUS Website Analysis - University Building Reference

## Overview

The National University of Singapore (NUS) website serves as the primary digital presence for Singapore's flagship university. It is clean, modern, and organized around clear user journeys: prospective students, current students, researchers, alumni, and the general public.

**Key Design Principles Observed:**

- Minimal, uncluttered header with hamburger menu
- Content-forward layout with strong visual hierarchy
- Social proof prominently displayed (rankings, Instagram feed, testimonials)
- Clear separation between academic offerings, research, and campus life
- Multi-language support (English/Chinese)

## KEY INSIGHTS FOR BUILDING YOUR UNIVERSITY

### 1. Website Architecture

- **Start with a clean, minimal header.** NUS uses just a logo, search, and hamburger menu. This keeps focus on content.
- **Organize around user journeys.** Separate clear paths for: Prospective Students, Current Students, Researchers, Alumni, General Public.
- **Use landing page as storytelling device.** NUS homepage is essentially a magazine: featured stories, President's message, social media feed, thought leadership.

### 2. Content Strategy

- **Lead with impact, not administration.** Every section opens with a human-centered statement about transformation and impact.
- **Use student stories extensively.** "Shape Your Future" section puts student voices front and center.
- **Social proof is critical.** Rankings, statistics, Instagram feed, testimonials from partner universities.
- **Separate sustainability as a dedicated initiative.** NUS has a full subsite, not just a page. This signals institutional priority.

### 3. Academic Presentation

- **Show breadth and depth together.** NUS displays "15 Colleges, Faculties & Schools" alongside "4,000+ courses" and detailed programme listings.
- **Emphasize global opportunities.** "8 in 10 undergraduates go on study-abroad programmes" is a powerful selling point.
- **Lifelong learning is a key differentiator.** 20-year enrolment validity, executive education, continuing education.

### 4. Research Presentation

- **Frame research as problem-solving.** "From blue skies research... to problem-driven research addressing global needs."
- **Show the ecosystem.** Research institutes, corporate labs, shared facilities, commercialization pathways.
- **Highlight partnerships.** Government, industry, academic collaborations.
- **Make research tangible.** Featured stories with clear applications (e.g., MOF membranes for energy efficiency).

### 5. Governance & Transparency

- **Publish leadership information openly.** Board of Trustees, Management, President biography, speeches.
- **Share institutional documents.** Annual reports, impact reports, statutes, codes of conduct.
- **Provide comprehensive contact information.** Every page has clear contact pathways.
